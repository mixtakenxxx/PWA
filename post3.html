NRG WIN CHAMPIONS PARIS 3-2

Oct 5 2025 5 Min read

In an exhilarating Grand Final at the Accor Arena in Paris, NRG outlasted Fnatic in a five-map thriller to clinch the 2025 world championship. NRG surged ahead with dominant performances on the first two maps — securing a 13–3 win on Corrode followed by a 13–6 victory on Lotus. Though Fnatic roared back with overtime and Ascent wins, taking the series to a decider, NRG held their nerve and closed out the final map, Sunset, 13–5 to lift the trophy with a 3–2 overall score.

The triumph carried added significance: NRG went undefeated throughout the tournament’s playoffs, and their in-game leader Ethan "Ethan" Arnold made history by becoming the first player to win the VALORANT Champions title twice — first in 2023, and now again in 2025 with NRG. Meanwhile, newcomer Brock "brawk" Somerhalder was named Finals MVP after standout performances throughout, including clutch rounds and strong stats that helped seal the win.

Reflecting on the triumph, NRG’s organisation hailed the win as the culmination of years of dedication — a “flawless” run from group stage to Grand Final, and a testament to their roster’s blend of veteran leadership and rising talent. For NRG, Champions Paris isn’t just a trophy — it is proof that even underdog teams can rise to the top with composure, teamwork, and clutch performances.
